Can We Predict A Review Score Based on Wording Alone?

A machine learning analysis regarding the effect of word-choice on Amazon review scores

Find Out More

Project Scope:

Will the relative frequency of negative words in Amazon's Tools reviewset hold predictive value concerning star rating?

What's classified as "Negative"?

What are "Negative Words"?

We started by selecting the most general set of negatives words with the belief that the aggregate negative word count and other ratios could be used on other categories of product reviews. The first set of data we tested, mainly relied on negative verb forms to like "cant'", won't, and didn't. We then researched to see if anyone else has created such a list. We found a list from the grammarly blog which had our basic list plus some adverbs. After running this list, we found that there were too many reviews that did not have any negative words but were obviously negative. so we looked at the reviews without any negative words and added words like "junk", "broken" and "returned" which gave us a much better result. We found a nice distribution when looking at average negative word count per rating review level.

Choosing the Tools Reviewset

There was a diverse selection of Amazon product categories to choose from, varying from Music, to Tools, to Digital EBook Purchases. We settled on the Tools Reviewset because of the relatively objective experience of using a tool.
